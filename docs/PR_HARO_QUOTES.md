- Don't pitch the product — pitch the insight. Product mention only in attribution

## Template 1 — Copy trading risks / pathologies

**Use when query mentions:** copy trading, social trading, mirror
trading, retail crypto losses, signal services, trading
education, "why do traders lose money"

```
The most overlooked risk in retail copy trading isn't picking the
wrong publisher — it's getting copied through their worst hour.

When a publisher takes a loss and revenge-trades on the next
entry at multiples of their normal size, every subscriber takes
the same oversized loss. The mirror is faithful; that's the
feature on a good day and the bug on a bad one.

The structural fix retail copy-trading platforms haven't
shipped yet is a curation layer that recommends a *team* of
publishers in form right now, rather than asking the user to bet
on one master. Picking a portfolio of disciplined sources is a
more forgiving frame than picking a single winner.
```

## Template 3 — Retail crypto mistakes / behavioral finance

**Use when query mentions:** retail crypto, behavioral finance,
investor mistakes, FOMO, panic selling, crypto for beginners

```
Three patterns blow up more retail crypto accounts than the rest
combined: revenge trading after a loss, leverage drift in
winning streaks, and silent strategy abandonment when a regime
changes. Notice they're all behavioral, not analytical — the
trader knows the right move and does the wrong one anyway.

The structural fix isn't education. Education is undefeated by
emotional state. The fix is delegation to a system that holds
your declared risk envelope when your willpower can't.

For most retail accounts under $50K, that means automated
position sizing, hard drawdown caps, and copy-trading from
disciplined sources rather than discretionary trading.
```

The regulatory question on autonomous trading agents isn't

## Tactical notes

**What makes a quote land:**
- Specific number ("1 in 8" beats "some")
- Counter-intuitive claim (reporters love "everyone thinks X but actually Y")
- Concrete anecdote without naming names
- Self-aware (don't claim to solve everything)

**What gets you cut:**
- Hedging ("could potentially possibly maybe")
- Marketing language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-gen")
- Anything that requires a reader to know what BottomUP is
- Quotes longer than 4 sentences

**Tracking:**

Maintain a sheet (Notion/Sheets) with:
- Date sent
- Reporter + outlet
- Query topic
- Template used
- Outcome (landed / cut / no response / "follow up later")

After 60 days, look at hit rate by template — kill the weak ones, double down on the strong.

## Variations

For each template, develop 2-3 variations over time so the same
phrasing doesn't appear twice in different outlets (reporters
sometimes Google check). Keep the data point and POV; vary the
opening line and metaphor.
