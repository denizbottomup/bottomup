'''BottomUP''', legally '''BottomUP, Inc.''', is an American
[[financial technology]] company headquartered in [[Wilmington,
Delaware]]. The company operates a [[social trading]] marketplace
where retail users mirror the trades of human traders, algorithmic
bots, and autonomous AI agents on [[cryptocurrency]] exchanges, with
each trading signal evaluated by a proprietary risk-audit system
called Foxy AI before it is executed on the subscriber's account.

== History ==

BottomUP was founded in 2024 as a [[Delaware corporation]]. The
company released a mobile application on [[iOS]] and [[Android (operating system)|Android]],
followed by a web product at bottomup.app.<ref name="company-site" />

== Product ==

BottomUP's product consists of three components:

* '''Marketplace.''' Subscribers can follow trade publishers — human
  traders, algorithmic bots, or autonomous [[AI agent]]s — who connect
  their cryptocurrency exchange accounts via [[Application programming interface|API]].
  When a publisher opens a position, the same position is opened on
  each subscriber's connected account in proportion to a copy ratio
  the subscriber sets.<ref name="company-site" />
* '''Foxy AI risk firewall.''' A signal-evaluation system that scores
  each incoming trade on a 0–100 scale against several inputs,
  including order-book depth, [[funding rate]]s, the publisher's
  historical pattern, and real-time news sentiment. Trades scoring
  below the subscriber's chosen threshold are blocked before
  execution.<ref name="company-site" />
* '''Bup.AI.''' A natural-language assistant that explains portfolio
  decisions in plain text rather than charts.<ref name="company-site" />

The product targets retail traders with portfolios in the range of
roughly $1,000 to $50,000.<ref name="company-site" />

== Coverage and availability ==

The mobile application is available worldwide on the iOS App Store
and Google Play Store. Copy-trading functionality is offered on
[[OKX]] and is not currently available to U.S. persons due to the
exchange's retail-customer restrictions.<ref name="company-site" />
Residents of OFAC-sanctioned regions are also ineligible.

The product interface is available in ten languages: English,
[[Turkish language|Turkish]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]],
[[Portuguese language|Portuguese]], [[Russian language|Russian]],
[[Vietnamese language|Vietnamese]], [[Indonesian language|Indonesian]],
[[Chinese language|Chinese]], [[Korean language|Korean]], and
[[Arabic language|Arabic]].<ref name="company-site" />

== Regulatory status ==

BottomUP is not a registered [[investment adviser]] in the United
States and does not provide investment advice. The company's terms
of service describe the platform as a [[software-as-a-service]]
product that routes orders through a connected exchange API on
behalf of the user.<ref name="company-site" />

## After submission

If the AfC reviewer rejects for non-notability, **don't resubmit
the same text**. Either:

1. Wait until you have stronger third-party coverage (typically 3+
   substantive articles in major business press), then resubmit; or
2. Pursue a [[Wikidata]] entry first (lower bar — just needs the
   company to exist and be verifiable), and let the Wikipedia
   article come later when notability is unambiguous.

A Wikidata entry alone gets BottomUP into the knowledge graph that
Google's Knowledge Panel and most LLMs (Claude, GPT, Gemini) pull
from, even without a full Wikipedia article. That's a high-leverage
intermediate step worth doing.
