# Twitter (X) — 30-day founder cadence

> **Owner:** Founder (Deniz)
> **Cadence:** 1 main thread or long post per day + 2-3 reply engagements
> **Goal:** Build crypto Twitter audience from cold start to 5-10K relevant followers in 90 days
> **Tone:** Confident but humble. Data-driven. Opinionated. Not promotional.

## Account hygiene first

- [ ] Bio: "Founder, [BottomUP](bottomup.app). Building AI-protected copy trading. Building Foxy AI: the risk firewall for retail crypto." (Use Twitter's bullet/break formatting)
- [ ] Pinned tweet: a thread with strongest insight/POV (use Day 1 thread once shipped)
- [ ] Profile photo: clean headshot, not logo
- [ ] Banner: bottomup.app screenshot or brand graphic
- [ ] Following: 200-300 crypto founders + traders + researchers + journalists. Build the algo's pattern of who you talk to.

## The 4 thread types

Mix these throughout the month. Don't do 5 in a row of one type.

1. **POV / opinion piece** — bold claim, defend it. ("Most copy traders lose money. Here's the real reason.")
2. **Data drop** — specific numbers from internal data. ("We audited 12,847 copy-trade signals last month. Here's what we found.")
3. **Behind-the-scenes** — building in public, lessons. ("3 things I got wrong building Foxy AI in 2024.")
4. **Industry commentary** — react to news/event. ("OKX just announced X. Here's what it means for retail.")

## Hook patterns that work on crypto Twitter

- "Most people in crypto get [X] wrong. Here's why:"
- "I've seen [N] [accounts/trades/setups]. The pattern is uncomfortable:"
- "The reason [common thing] doesn't work isn't what you think:"
- "[Number]. That's how many [thing] [happened]. A thread:"
- "Hot take: [contrarian statement] (defending below)"

## Daily routine (15 min)

- 9am: post the day's thread (drafted night before)
- Throughout day: 2-3 substantive replies to threads in your space (not 1-line replies — 2-3 sentence engagement)
- Evening: scan tomorrow's calendar entry, draft next day's thread

## What to avoid

- **Self-promotion fatigue** — never post "check out our product" tweets. Earned mention only.
- **Engagement bait** — "comment 'YES' if you agree" stuff. Crypto Twitter sniffs it out.
- **Inflated claims** — say "we" when "we" did it. Don't claim industry-firsts that aren't.
- **Picking fights for visibility** — ratio-attempts feel cheap, don't compound.
- **Daily takes on every news event** — only react when you have a real POV. Empty reactions dilute.

## Tracking

Log weekly:
- Followers gained
- Best-performing thread (impressions, reposts, replies)
- Worst-performing (kill that pattern)
- Replies-to-DMs-to-bottomup.app conversion (use UTM)

After 90 days: 5-10K followers if topic-relevant. Inbound DMs from journalists, fund managers, crypto OGs is the real signal — not follower count.

## Reuse → blog

Every 4-5 threads, expand the strongest one into a blog post on bottomup.app/blog. Twitter audience finds it on Twitter, organic search audience finds it on Google. Same insight, two distribution channels.
