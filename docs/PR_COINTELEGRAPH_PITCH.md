## Why this article fits Cointelegraph

- Cointelegraph Opinion runs founder/operator POVs that take a clear position. Article 2 is structured exactly that way: it disagrees with the "AI trading" framing as a single product category and re-frames it as four distinct things.
- The piece is data-light enough to be readable but specific enough to feel non-generic — the assistive-vs-autonomous distinction is the kind of editorial argument they pick up.
- We're not pitching the product. We're pitching an industry observation that happens to mention BottomUP once at the end.

## What to expect

- **Lead time:** Cointelegraph Opinion typically responds within 1–3 weeks for cold founder pitches. No response in 14 days = move on; pitch CryptoSlate or Decrypt.
- **Acceptance rate:** Cold pitches without a prior relationship land at maybe 15–25%. The headline framing matters; ours is differentiated.
- **If accepted:** they'll request light edits. Take them. Do not push back on style changes; push back only if a fact is changed.
- **If rejected:** ask for the specific reason. If "not for us right now," the piece is recyclable for The Block or Decrypt with the byline kept.
- **Embargo:** the 14-day exclusivity offer is standard. Do not pitch the same article to multiple outlets at the same time — burns trust if discovered.
